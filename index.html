The Periods of Classical Music

What is commonly referred to as "Classical Music" is divided into several periods that blend into each other.

Listed below are the divisions of Classical music by eras, their proposed time frames, and a selection of notable composers (too numerous to name in this exercise) from each era:

  1. Medieval Era (500–1420)

    • Hildegard of Bingen (1098–1179)
    • Léonin (1150s–1201)
    • Guillaume de Machaut (1300–1377)

  2. Renaissance Era (1400–1600)

    • John Dunstable (1390–1453)
    • Guillame Dufay (1397–1474)
    • Giovanni Pierluigi da Palestrina (1525–1594)

  3. Baroque Era (1600–1750)

    • Johann Sebastian Bach (1685–1750)
    • George Frederic Handel (1685–1759)
    • Barbara Strozzi (1619–1677)

  4. Galant Music Era (1720s–1770s)

    • Johann Joachim Quantz (1697–1773)
    • Carl Friedrich Abel (1723–1787)
    • Tommaso Traetta (1727–1779)

  5. Classical Era (1750–1820)

    • Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart (1756–1791)
    • Ludwig van Beethoven (1770–1827)
    • Franz Joseph Haydn (1732–1809)
    • Franz Schubert (1797–1828)
    • Marianna Martines (1744–1812)

  6. Romantic Era (c. 1800–1910)

    • Frédéric Chopin (1810–1849)
    • Franz Liszt (1811–1886)
    • Richard Wagner (1813–1883)
    • Johannes Brahms (1833–1897)
    • Pyotr Ilyich Tchaikovsky (1840–1893)

  7. Modernist Era (1890–1975)

    • Irving Berlin (1888–1989)
    • Ruth Crawford-Seeger (1901–1953)
    • Aaron Copland (1900–1990)

  8. Contemporary/Postmodern Era (1950–present)

    • Karlheinz Stockhausen (1928–2007)
    • Yi Chen (1953– )
    • Jennifer Higdon (1962– )
